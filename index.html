Strengthening the Frontlines: Nigeria’s Innovations in COVID-19 Contact Tracing and Digital Response Systems

At the height of the COVID-19 pandemic, Nigeria was in a race against time to control the spread of the virus and protect its citizens. Behind the scenes, public health professionals worked tirelessly to build systems that could track the outbreak in real time. Among these leaders was Clara Oguji, a digital health strategist whose work played a pivotal role in modernizing Nigeria’s contact tracing and outbreak response efforts.

With over a decade of experience leading data-driven health programs, Clara was deployed to the national and state COVID-19 Command Centers, where she provided technical support in digital contact tracing, training, and real-time data analysis. As new tools were introduced to enhance Nigeria’s surveillance capacity, Clara led the charge in ensuring that frontline teams could adopt and use these technologies effectively.

“I knew that building trust and digital capacity among health workers would be just as important as the tools themselves,” Clara noted. She conducted intensive training sessions for contact tracing teams in the Federal Capital Territory and supported coordination across several high-burden states. Her focus on simplifying digital workflows helped reduce delays in case investigations and improved communication between local and national teams.

Beyond contact tracing, Clara also developed specialized data collection tools that captured behavioral insights, including community reasons for vaccine hesitancy. These tools enabled public health authorities to adapt strategies based on real-time community feedback, improving the reach and effectiveness of the national vaccination campaign.

She also played a key role in integrating COVID-19 surveillance into Nigeria’s broader health data systems. By linking pandemic tracking with maternal and child health monitoring platforms, Clara helped ensure that pandemic-related disruptions could be identified and addressed, especially in vulnerable populations.

The impact of her work didn’t end with the emergency phase. Many of the digital practices and data tools she supported have since been adapted for use in other public health areas, from cholera surveillance to maternal health interventions. Her contributions have helped build a more agile, data-responsive health system that continues to serve Nigeria today.

“The pandemic challenged our systems, but it also gave us the opportunity to build smarter, more resilient ones,” Clara reflected.

Through her dedication, technical expertise, and passion for data-driven health solutions, Clara Oguji has emerged as one of the key figures behind Nigeria’s innovative response to COVID-19. Her work is a testament to the power of digital transformation in public health—and a model for future outbreak response in Nigeria and beyond
